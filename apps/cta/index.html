Pixel art began as a constraint...

...defined by hardware limitations.

Mega Drive/Genesis, 1988

64 KB VRAM, 6-bit(-3) color (9-bit palette)

SNES, 1990

64 KB VRAM, 8-bit color (15-bit palette)

Game Boy Color, 1998

16 KB VRAM, 6-bit(-8) color (15-bit palette)

Today, we bring pixel art to physical displays...

...with MQTT feeding live art from the web...

...and a platform built for permanence, not growth.

This is an open project. Join us.

Why Makapix exists

Most social platforms optimize for engagement and growth. They control what you see, sell ads against your work, and treat creators as content suppliers.

Makapix takes a different approach:

  • No ads, ever. No subscriptions, no paywalls, no hidden costs.
  • No algorithms. Your feed is chronological. You control what you see.
  • Open-source. The code is transparent. The platform is built to last.
  • Built for craft. Publish work you're proud of. Document your projects. Connect with a community that values patience over virality.

Your work stays yours. The platform stays open.
